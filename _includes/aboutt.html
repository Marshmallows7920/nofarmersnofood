November 26, 2021 will mark 1 year anniversary of the Indian Farmers’ protests -
largest protest ever held in the world’s history, which, despite getting international coverage and attention from media, has still not been able to draw the attention of Bharatiya Janata Party led Central Government of India.

In the September of 2020, the Indian Government passed 3 bills –

[1] The Farmers’ Produce Trade and Commerce Act link
[2] Farmers Agreement on Price Assurance and Farm Services Act link
[3] Essential Commodities Act link



On September 20, 2020,

Prime Minister Narendra Modi referred to the bills as a watershed moment in the history of Indian agriculture and stated the bills will “ensure a complete transformation of the agriculture sector” and empower tens of millions of farmers. “Farm bills and labour bills are very important steps in the right direction. They have the potential to have more labour market flexibility, providing greater social security to workers and more formalization of the labour market. In the case of agriculture, having a much more integrated market, creating competition, having farmers getting a greater share of the price that finally the retail price that’s paid. So that helps with rural incomes.” source

The Ordinance was passed in Rajya Sabha by a voice vote, which was stated as questionable and unparliamentary by many for such a critical bill. source

Ever since the three bills were brought into question, several farmer unions and organisations from the states of Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Telangana, Maharashtra, and many others have been openly critical and protesting the three monstrous bills, stating that “the recent laws enacted at the Centre will dismantle the minimum support price (MSP) system. Over time big corporate houses will dictate terms and farmers will end up getting less for their crops, they argue. Farmers fear that with the virtual disbanding of the mandi system, they will not get an assured price for their crops and the ’arthiyas’ —commission agents who also pitch in with loans for them — will be out of business.” source
However, the union government of ‘world’s largest democracy’ is unwilling to be considerate about its people who are pertaining to an industry which employs 54.6% of the Indian working population.



Here’s a brief account of the major incidents that caught the eyes of Indian media ever since the bill was introduced:
  1. June 5, 2020 The Union Government endorsed the three farm bills, post which several farmer unions raised their voices against the bills.
  2. September 10, 2020 Haryana Police lathi charged the farmers protesting the three farm bills. link
  3. September 14th, 2020 The Ordinance is brought to Parliament. link
  4. September 17, 2020 Lok Sabha passed the farm bills. link
  5. September 20,2020 The ordinance was passed in Rajya Sabha by voice notes, despite the unprecedented uproar and protest in the parliament. link This was also the first time in history when Rajya Sabha TV feed was cut during the proceedings. link
  6. September 24 -26, 2020 Farmers in Punjab announced a 3 day ‘rail roko’ agitation against the farm bills. link
  7. September 27, 2020 President Ram Nath Kovind gave his assent to farm bills, which were notified in the Gazette of India and the farm laws came into existence. link
  8. November 3, 2020 30 farmers’ organisations demonstrated a nationwide road blockage or ‘chakka jam’ against the farm laws. link
  9. November 25, 2020 After periodic protests against the new farm laws, farmers’ unions in Punjab and Haryana gave the call for a ‘Delhi Chalo’ movement. The Delhi Police, however, rejected their request to march to the capital city citing Covid-19 protocols. link
  10. November 26, 2020 The largest march in the world’s history took place when thousands of farmers from Punjab came down on the streets and proceeded towards the national capital of Delhi. In order to stop the farmers, Delhi police used brutal force, water canons and barricades, while also lathi charging several unarmed farmers and leaving them injured. link
  11. November 28, 2020 Home Minister and Bharatiya Janata Party leader Amit Shah offered to hold talks with farmers as soon as they vacate Delhi borders and move to the designated protest site in Burari. However, farmers rejected his offer, demanding to hold the protest at Jantar Mantar.
  12. The umbrella organisation – Samyukt Kisaan Morcha had jointly decided not to shift to Burari on Saturday. “What the Government of India is keen on doing is to defend what it has already done. This is apparent from the fact that the government goes on saying that it will ‘explain’ the benefits to the farmers, without presenting any concrete agenda even once that they are fully ready to go back on what has been enacted,” said the farmers’ statement. link
  13. December 3, 2020 The government held first round of talks with representatives of farmers but the meeting remained inconclusive. link
  14. December 8, 2020 Farmers gave a call for ‘Bharath Bandh’ – nationwide shut down. link
  15. December 9, 2020 Farmer leaders rejected the Union government’s proposal to amend the three contentious laws and vowed to further intensify their agitation until the laws are repealed. link
  16. December 11, 2020 Bharatiya Kisaan Union (BKU) moved to the apex court against the three farm laws. The Union said the court should direct the government to strengthen the existing Agriculture Produce Market Committee (APMC) system for sale of farm produce at a good bargain. link
  17. December 16, 2020 On the 21st day of the protest, Supreme Court of India announced it may constitute a panel having representatives of the government and farmer unions to end the deadlock over the controversial agriculture laws. link
  18. December 21, 2020 Farmers held a one-day relay hunger strike at all the protest sites across various borders of Delhi. link
  19. December 30, 2020 Sixth round of talks between government and farmer leaders saw some headway as the Centre agreed to exempt farmers from stubble burning penalty and drop changes in Electricity Amendment Bill, 2020. link
  20. January 4, 2021 Seventh round of talks between government and farmer leaders also remained inconclusive with the Centre not agreeing to repeal the farm laws. link
  21. January 11, 2021 After agreeing to hear the petitions challenging the new laws, the Supreme Court rapped the Centre for its handling of the farmers’ protest. The top court said it would constitute a committee headed by a former Chief Justice of India to resolve the deadlock. link
  22. January 12, 2021 The Supreme Court stayed the implementation of the three contentious farm laws and set up a four-member committee to make recommendations on the legislations after listening to all stakeholders link
  23. January 12, 2021 Dalit labour activist Nodeep Kaur was arrested by Sonipat police on charges of attempt to murder, rioting, and assault to deter a public servant from discharge of his duty under the Indian Penal Code, among others. Kaur, a member of the Mazdoor Adhikar Sangathan, claimed she was “targeted and falsely implicated” in the case as she was successful in generating massive support for the ongoing farmer movement against the three new farm laws. link
  24. January 26, 2021 On India’s Republic Day, thousands of protestors clashed with the police during the tractor parade called by farmer unions on January 26, demanding a repeal of the laws. After several protestors from Singhu and Ghazipur borders changed their route, they marched towards Central Delhi’s ITO and Red Fort, where police resorted to teargas shelling and lathi charge while some farmers vandalised public property and attacked police personnel.
  25. 26th continuedOne protester died in the chaos. A big uproar has been raised by the Indian government and some sections of the media over the incidents of January 26. The government used the incidents as an excuse to tighten its noose on the entire movement. However, things were not as polarised as claimed by sections of the media.
  26. 26th continuedMany of the people participating in the tractor parade on January 26 conducted their march peacefully. But a small section of the protesting farmers, not part of the 32 unions involved in negotiations with the government, broke the first barricades and marched towards the Red Fort. It was projected that an unruly mob was taking over the capital on Republic Day.
  27. 26th continuedThe sacrifices farmers have been making for more than two months were outrightly disregarded. Numerous farmers lost their lives during this peaceful protest either due to suicide, extreme cold conditions or health reasons, for which the government has neither apologised nor expressed concern. link
  28. January 28, 2021 Tensions rose at Delhi’s Ghazipur border after the administration in neighbouring UP’s Ghaziabad district issued orders for protesting farmers to vacate the site by night. By evening, as police in anti-riot gear started spreading out at the site, the protesters camped there and their leaders, including BKU’s Rakesh Tikait, said they would not leave. link
  29. February 4, 2021 The government slammed “celebrities and others” for their comments in support of farmer protests, calling them “neither accurate nor responsible”. This came after pop icon Rihanna, teenage climate activist Greta Thunberg and lawyer-author Meena Harris, niece of US Vice President Kamala Harris, spoke out on the farmer protests. link
  30. February 5, 2021 The cyber-crime cell of the Delhi Police has registered an FIR on charges of “sedition”, “criminal conspiracy” and “promoting hatred” against the creators of a ‘toolkit’ on farmer protests, which was shared by Thunberg. The 18-year-old deleted the original tweet on Wednesday but tweeted a revised toolkit on Wednesday night link
  31. February 6, 2021 Protesting farmers held a nationwide ‘Chakka Jam’, or road blockade, for three hours from 12 noon to 3 pm. While several roads across Punjab and Haryana were blocked during that time, elsewhere the ‘chakka jam’ protest evoked a scattered response. link
  32. February 14, 2021 The Delhi Police’ arrested 21-year-old climate activist Disha Ravi for allegedly “editing” the toolkit shared by Thunberg link
  33. February 18, 2021 Samyukta Kisan Morcha (SKM), the umbrella body of farmer unions spearheading the agitations last week, called for a nationwide ‘rail roko’ protest. Trains were stopped, cancelled and rerouted in places around the country. However, a spokesperson for the national transporter said there was negligible or minimal impact on railway services due to the ‘rail roko’ agitation. He said majority of the zonal railways reported no incident due to the protest. link
  34. February 23, 2021 22-year-old activist Disha Ravi was granted bail by a sessions court in Delhi.
  35. February 25, 2021 Dalit labour activist Nodeep Kaur was released from prison after she was granted bail by the Punjab and Haryana High Court. Kaur’s bail plea came up for hearing before the bench of Justice Avneesh Jhingan link
  36. March 05, 2021 The Punjab Vidhan Sabha passed a resolution asking for the unconditional withdrawal of the farm laws in the interest of the farmers and Punjab, and to continue with the existing system of MSP-based government procurement of food grains link
  37. April 15, 2021 Haryana Deputy Chief Minister Dushyant Chautala wrote to Prime Minister Narendra Modi urging him to resume talks with farmers who are protesting at Delhi’s borders and reach an “amicable conclusion” to the impasse over the farm laws. link
  38. May 27, 2021 Farmers observed a ‘black day’ to mark six months of the agitation and burned effigies of the government. Though the crowds at the three borders have thinned, farm leaders said that agitation will continue till 2024 if their demands are not met. Bharatiya Kisan Union leader Rakesh Tikait, during a press conference, also reiterated that farmers will call off the protest only once the three farm laws are repealed. link
  39. June 5, 2021 Protesting farmers observed Sampoorn Krantikari Diwas (total revolution day) to mark the first year of the promulgation of the Farm Laws link
  40. June 26, 2021 Farmers marched to Delhi to mark seven months of protest against the farm laws. Samyukta Kisan Morcha (SKM) claimed that farmers were detained in states like Haryana, Punjab, Karnataka, Uttarakhand, Madhya Pradesh, and Telangana during the protests. link
  41. July 2021 About 200 protesting farmers started a parallel “Monsoon Session”, Kisan Sansad, near the Parliament House here on Thursday, condemning the three farm laws. Members of Opposition parties staged protests in front of the Mahatma Gandhi statue inside the House complex.
  42. July 2021 They urged the Centre to withdraw the farm laws and accept the demands of farmers. Congress leader Rahul Gandhi also reached Parliament on a tractor to express solidarity with protesting farmers. Parliament proceedings were adjourned repeatedly as the Opposition cornered the Centre over farm laws among other issues. link
  43. August 7, 2021 Leaders of 14 opposition parties met at Parliament House and decided to visit Kisan Sansad at Delhi’s Jantar Mantar, where a select group of farmer leaders have been holding a Kisan Sansad (farmers’ parliament) since July 22 to mark seven months of the protests at Delhi’s border points against the laws.
  44. Indian National Congress leader Rahul Gandhi and the others reiterated that the three controversial laws should be withdrawn. “Today all opposition parties together decided to support the farmers and demand the withdrawal of the three farm laws,” Gandhi said. “We want to give our entire support to all the farmers of the country.” link
  45. August 28, 2021 The agitation against farm laws enacted last year returned to the spotlight when Haryana Police cracked down on farmers in Karnal, leaving several injured in a lathicharge at the Bastara toll plaza on the national highway. The farmers were protesting a BJP meeting on the forthcoming panchayat polls — it was being chaired by Chief Minister Manohar Lal Khattar. link
  46. August 28 2021 They showed black flags to vehicles carrying BJP leaders and even tried to block their way. But due to heavy police presence, they could not stop the vehicles and kept raising slogans. When they refused to lift the highway blockade, police used force, charging at farmers with lathis and leaving several injured – they were taken to local hospitals by fellow protesters.
  47. The farmers started the protest demanding Ayush Sinha’s suspension and registration of an FIR against him ever since the lathicharge. A 2018 batch IAS officer, he was caught on camera on August 28, instructing policemen to beat up protesting farmers and not to let anyone breach the security cordon without a “broken head”. link
  48. September 5, 2021 Challenging the BJP-led NDA with just months to go for the UP elections, farm leaders driving the movement against the Centre’s new agricultural laws staged a major show of strength in Muzaffarnagar, announcing in front of thousands of farmers their plan to campaign against the ruling party in the state. link
  49. September 7-9, 2021 Farmers reach Karnal in large numbers and laid siege to the mini-Secretariat. The farmers put forth three primary demands including Rs 25 lakh compensation to the family of Kajal and a government job for his relative, compensation of Rs 2 lakh each for those injured in the lathicharge and registration of a criminal case and stern action against Karnal SDM Ayush Sinha and police personnel responsible for the lathicharge. link
  50. September 11, 2021 After 5 days of standoff between the farmers and Karnal District Administration, the Haryana government agreed to conduct a probe by a retired judge of the Punjab and Haryana High Court into the August 28 police lathicharge on farmers at the Bastara toll plaza, and send former Karnal SDM Ayush Sinha on leave till completion of the inquiry.
  51. The standoff ended following extensive talks between farmer unions led by Gurnam Singh Chadhuni and the government side led by Additional Chief Secretary Devender Singh. They later held a press conference to announce the agreement. link
  52. September 22, 2021 Farmers’ Protest completed 300 days. Samyukt Kisan Morcha (SKM) said the movement is a testimony to the will and determination of lakhs of farmers across the country that would only grow stronger. link
  53. September 27, 2021 Farmers called out for a 10-hour strike and blocked traffic to mark the 1-year anniversary since the farm laws came into existence. link
  54. October 2, 2021 Water canons used when farmers protested at Haryana CM Manohar Lal Khattar’s Karnal residence. link
  55. October 3, 2021 During a peaceful demonstration at Tikunia, eight people, including four farmers, died in violence that broke out in Uttar Pradesh's Lakhimpur Kheri ahead of a visit by Deputy Chief Minister Keshav Prasad Maurya. The violence erupted after a car belonging to Ashish Mishra, son of Minister of State (MoS) for Home Ajay Mishra, allegedly ran over the farmers who were protesting the Centre's three farm laws ahead of an event in Tikunia.
  56. Four farmers were killed in the incident. This led to clashes between the protesters and BJP workers, which led to the death of four more people, from the BJP's convoy. link
  57. October 26, 2021 Samyukt Kisaan Morcha calls for a nationwide protest, while also demanding arrest and dismissal of Union Minister of State for Home Affairs Ajay Mishra in connection with the Lakhimpur Kheri incident. link
  58. November 9, 2021 To mark 1 year anniversary of the farmers’ protest, the Samyukt Kisaan Morcha announced Tractor March every day from November 29, 2021, wherein 500 selected farmer volunteers will be moving to parliament daily to register their protest against the central government and its apathy towards the misery of India’s farmers. link
